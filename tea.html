What's tea?

Tea, an ancient and revered beverage, has captivated hearts and palates for millennia. Originating from the leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant, it has evolved into a diverse array of flavors and traditions worldwide. From the bright, grassy notes of green tea to the robust, malty depths of black tea, each cup offers a journey through terroirs and craftsmanship. Beyond its taste, tea embodies a sense of ritual and mindfulness. It serves as a moment of calm in a bustling world, inviting reflection and contemplation. Whether enjoyed in the tranquil solitude of a morning ritual or shared in the company of friends, tea weaves a thread of connection through cultures and generations. With its myriad health benefits, from antioxidants to calming properties, tea stands not only as a delightful refreshment, but also as a balm for the soul. From the delicate grace of white tea to the bold complexities of oolong, the world of tea is a treasure trove of tastes waiting to be explored.

Types of Tea

There are many types of tea, and they all come from the leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant. The differences between these teas arise from factors such as processing, oxidation, and region. Here are some of the main types of tea:

Types
  1. Green Tea: Made from unoxidized leaves, green tea is known for its fresh, grassy flavor. It's rich in antioxidants and is believed to have various health benefits.
  2. Black Tea: Fully oxidized leaves give black tea its dark color and robust flavor. It's the most commonly consumed type of tea worldwide.
  3. Oolong Tea: This tea falls between green and black tea in terms of oxidation. It has a diverse range of flavors, from light and floral to dark and rich.
  4. White Tea: Made from young leaves and buds that are minimally processed, white tea has a delicate and subtle flavor profile.
  5. Pu-erh Tea: This aged tea hails from China's Yunnan province. It can be either raw (sheng) or ripe (shou), with distinct fermentation processes that result in unique flavors.
  6. Herbal Tea: Technically not a true tea, herbal infusions are made from various herbs, fruits, flowers, and spices. Examples include chamomile, peppermint, and hibiscus.
  7. Yellow Tea: A rare and delicate tea, yellow tea is processed in a way similar to green tea but with an additional step to mellow the flavor.
  8. Jasmine Tea: Typically made by infusing green tea leaves with jasmine blossoms, resulting in a fragrant and floral brew.
  9. Chai: A spiced tea originating from India, chai typically includes black tea mixed with spices like cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, and cloves.
  10. Rooibos Tea: Originating from South Africa, rooibos is a herbal tea made from the leaves of the Aspalathus linearis plant. It has a naturally sweet and nutty flavor.
  11. Flavored Teas: These can be any type of tea infused with various flavors, such as fruit, floral, or spices.
